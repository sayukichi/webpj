2. You have the right to know about the effects and risks associated with your diagnosis, testing and treatment of the disease, as well as the availability of other treatments, how you are being cared for, and the course of the disease.
If you do not wish to know about your diagnosis, after approving of the “advantages and disadvantages of not knowing,”　 you may exercise your right to not be informed, and request that a person you trust receive any explanations on your behalf.
